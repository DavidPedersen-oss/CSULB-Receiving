Step-by-Step Guide for Receiving Clerks

  1. Pick the delivery type. At the top of "New Email," choose Package, Letter/Mail, or Pallet/Freight depending on what came in. If it's small enough to hand-deliver normally, it's a Package or Letter. If it's too big, heavy, or bulky for standard delivery, choose Pallet/Freight.
  2. Find the recipient. Type the name on the package into the search box. Click their name when it pops up — their email fills in automatically. If they don't show up in the list, just type their email in manually.
  3. Find their department. Type the department name (or the person's college/office, if you know it) into the department search box and click the right match. This automatically adds their ASM (Administrative Services Manager) as a Cc on the email so the department knows a package arrived. If you also know their office coordinator, you can add that name/email too — the tool remembers it for next time you pick that department.
  4. Fill in the details. Add the vendor name and tracking number if you have them. For Pallet/Freight, you must also check at least one reason (size, weight, or bulky) and remember to attach a photo of the item before sending.
  5. Click "Generate Email." You'll see a preview of exactly what will be sent — double check the recipient and Cc addresses.
  6. Click "Open in Outlook." This opens a new email draft in Outlook with everything already filled in. Attach a photo if needed (for pallets), give it one last look, and hit Send.
💡 Tip: if "Open in Outlook" doesn't do anything, click "Copy email body" instead, then paste it into a new email you start manually.
